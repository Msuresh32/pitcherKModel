How It Works
What you are looking at.

The model projects each starting pitcher's strikeout total from matchup, opponent contact profile, park, and recent form, then compares that projection to the posted line. This page filters to one slice: plays where the projection clears the line by at least half a strikeout, the higher conviction end of the model.

Across 472 such plays in the 2025 regular season, flat staking returned +10.26% ROI and grew a $10,000 bankroll to $14,842. Sizing the same plays with quarter Kelly grew it to $17,646, a 76% return, at the cost of larger swings.

Average edge per play was about 14.8% at an average price near even money. Every result here is graded against the actual strikeout total, wins and losses both, with no plays removed after the fact.
